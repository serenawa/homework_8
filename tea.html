Basic Dough
1/2 cup Unsalted Butter (room temp)
2/3 cup Powdered Sugar
2 Egg Yolks
2 cups Cake Flour
1/2 cup Almond Flour

Matcha Dough
2 1/2 tbsp Matcha Powder

Earl Grey Dough
2 1/2 tbsp Crushed Earl Grey Tea

1      The dough must be frozen quite stiff otherwise you won’t be able to slice it neatly.

2      You can add any flavor you want! Just use the basic dough and add anything: cocao powder for checkered cookies, lavendar, dried fruits, chocolate chips, the list goes on!

3      Make sure you transfer to a wire rack soon after taking the biscuits out of the oven, if you leave them on the baking tray too long the biscuits will be chewy instead of crispy.

fun fact: these biscuits are also called ice box cookies because you have to freeze the dough in order to slice it!

Instructions: overview

Basic Dough

Cream 1/2 cup room tem butter and 2/3 cup powdered sugar together with an electric mixer.

Add 2 egg yolks and mix by hand with whisk. Don’t worry if it looks slighty curdled: if your egg yolk came straight from the fridge, the temperature will cause slight curdling in the butter.

Add all dry ingredients (2 cups cake flower, 1/2 cup almond flour) and fold gently with spatula.

Flavored Doughs

Split dough in half. One half will be for the matcha and the other half for earl grey.

In one half of the dough, add the 2 1/2 tbsp matcha powder and gently fold with spatula.

With the other half, add the 2 1/2 tbsp crushed early grey tea leaves and fold gently with spatula.

Wrap the doughs individually in plastic wrap and shape to a cylindar.

Freeze for 1-2 hours.

Baking

Preheat oven to 350 F.

Take the dough out of the freezer once it’s solid. Slice the dough width-wise so you get circles each time you slice the dough.

Place biscuits on parchment paper on a baking tray and bake for 14-15 minutes.

Transfer to wire rack to cool as soon as possible.

Congratulations, you’ve finished making tea bisuits!
